Different Types Of Computers

Supercomputers

Supercomputers are the most powerful, expensive, and fastest computers available. They are utilized for specialized tasks that involves a lot of mathematical computations. To increase speed, they contain multiple CPUs that work in parallel processing. Their applications include nuclear energy research, animated graphics, and weather forecasting.

Mainframe Computers

Mainframe computers can support from hundreds to thousands of users simultaneously. They are huge, powerful, and expensive computers that are utilized in banks and government organizations where many of their users exchange data and applications.

Mini Computers

Compared to microcomputers, mini computers are more powerful and flexible that are used for a variety of purposes. They are a smaller and more compact version of the mainframe computer that can perform multiple tasks. They are used by multiple users simultaneously since they are more powerful than microcomputers.

Server

Servers are more robust and powerful than personal computers since they are designed to manage numerous tasks and high request volumes simultaneously. They are specialized systems made to provide data, resources, or services to other clients through a network. They are used for web hosting and support cloud computing.

Workstations

Workstations are considered to be high-end microcomputers. Workstations are used as standalone systems and are typically single-user computers. They are employed in software development, engineering applications such as computer-aided design (CAD) and other applications that require a moderate level of processing power and high-quality graphics.

Micro Computers

Microcomputers are the most widely used type of computer. They are known as personal computers and are typically single-user devices. Compared to the other kinds of computers, they are less expensive and bulky. Different types of microcomputers include desktop, laptop, notebook, smartphones, and embedded computers.
